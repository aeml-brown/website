Artibeus jamaicensis

Video: Jorn Cheney

Common name: Jamaican Fruit Bat

In this clip, we see a Jamaican fruit bat in one of our wind tunnels performing a chandelle, a maneuver that combines a sharp 180 degree turn with a climb. As the animal banks to the right, the dramatic billowing of its membrane wings is clearly visible (00:18 - 00:19) — note how the curvature of the trailing edge changes dynamically with the position of the limbs and digits relative to the animal’s body. The bright spots in the corner are reflections from the powerful lights required for high-speed video.

Shot at Brown University, Providence RI
