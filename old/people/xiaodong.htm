Research interests

My primary research interests are physical and numerical modeling for studies of complex turbulent flows, laser based non-intrusive measurement techniques (PIV, LIF, etc.), flow visualization techniques, and biological fluid mechanics. I developed a 3D LIF (Laser Induced Fluorescence) system for my PhD thesis and successfully applied the system to near field study of wastewater discharges and water quality in water storage tanks. Since May 2004 I started working for Dr. Jeannette Yen in School of Biology at Georgia Tech who is investigating the small-scale biological-chemical-physical interactions in the marine zooplankton. My work has focused on flows generated by different copepods. I joined a great team at Brown University in May 2005. My current work at Brown is focusing on mechanics of bat flight.
