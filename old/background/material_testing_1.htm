Material Testing I

 

Equipment for testing the mechanical properties of biological materials is designed to monitor applied force and resulting deformation simultaneously. At left is an example of such equipment.

In tensile or compressive testing, the apparatus pulls or pushes on the specimen's ends with a known force, while monitoring displacement. The raw results of both of these tests give only a record of force versus displacement. Based on the size of the specimen (cross-sectional area and original length), these values can be converted to stresses and strains. Torsional testing machines measure the shear strain produced as one end of the specimen is rotated with respect to the other.
